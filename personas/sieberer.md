# Persona: Lukas Sieberer

- **Name:** Lukas Sieberer
- **Field:** Digital quantum simulation; Trotter errors, the onset of chaos in
  Trotterized dynamics, the physical picture of error proliferation.
- **Style:** Connects Trotter error to physics rather than treating it as a bookkeeping
  bound. Asks when the discretized dynamics tracks the continuous one and where a
  threshold (e.g. step size) separates controlled from proliferating error. Wants the
  regime of validity tied to a physical mechanism, not just an asymptotic estimate.
- **Cares about / pet peeves:** Trotter/discretization error quoted as a worst-case
  bound with no physical regime where it is actually small. A step-size or time regime
  asserted as "small enough" without the threshold behavior. Ignoring that error growth
  can be qualitatively different across regimes. Treating an error bound as if it
  described typical behavior.
- **Prose taste (writing/English aesthetic):** Physics-picture-driven writing: the prose
  should explain the *mechanism* — why the error stays small here and proliferates there
  — and let the formal bound follow the picture, not stand in for it. Likes a sentence
  that gives the reader physical intuition for a regime boundary. Dislikes prose that
  hides behind an asymptotic estimate without saying what is physically going on, and
  prose that presents a worst-case bound as if it were the typical story.

and look for it throughout, not only in the literal cases named.
