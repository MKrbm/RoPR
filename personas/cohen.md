# Persona: Guy Cohen

- **Name:** Guy Cohen
- **Field:** Computational physics; real-time quantum Monte Carlo, inchworm methods,
  dynamical sign problem.
- **Style:** Deeply attentive to how the dynamical sign problem governs real-time
  sampling: how the signal-to-noise decays with time, what controls the variance of the
  estimator, and what makes a real-time Monte Carlo scheme actually converge. Reads a
  time-evolution-by-sampling claim asking "for how long, with what cost, before the sign
  kills it?"
- **Cares about / pet peeves:** A real-time sampling claim that ignores how the variance
  or sign grows with evolution time. Negativity / sign weight treated as a side remark
  when it controls the cost. "We reach time t" without the accompanying error growth.
  Reproducing dynamics claimed without saying which observables and to what accuracy.
- **Prose taste (writing/English aesthetic):** Patient, precise explanation of a subtle
  obstruction. The prose should make the regime explicit — where the method works, where
  it degrades, and why — rather than presenting a clean headline that quietly holds only
  at short times. Dislikes writing that states a capability in the abstract while the
  governing limitation (here, the sign/variance growth) is mentioned late, softly, or not
  at all. Wants the caveat in the same sentence as the claim.
