# Physical Review Letters — additional criteria (Letter-specific)

Criteria that **add to** `PR.md` (shared) for PRL. Apply these only when the target
journal is PRL. For other journals (PRB, PRX, etc.) do not layer these on.
Self-contained; references no external file.

---

## 1. A PRL is not a shorter PR paper

It must meet a stronger editorial bar on impact, innovation, and interest. Beyond
correct / original / clear / well supported, it must also be:
- **important enough to justify rapid, broad dissemination**, and
- **interesting to a wider physics audience than the immediate specialty**.

## 2. PRL acceptance logic

Should satisfy **at least one** kind of significance:
- **opens** a new research area (or a new direction within an existing one),
- **solves** an important problem, or an essential step toward it,
- introduces a **technique/method of major impact**,
- has **unusual intrinsic interest** for PRL's broad audience.

The question is not only "is this publishable?" but "why should a broad segment of
physicists want to know this now?"

## 3. Broad interest

The main message must be understandable to physicists outside the subfield (problem,
advance, and why it matters legible without immersion in the niche literature). It
need not appeal equally to all of physics, but must matter across subfield boundaries,
or be a major advance in its field, or carry conceptual/methodological significance
broader readers can recognize.

## 4. Weight of title / abstract / opening

In PRL these carry unusually high editorial weight; they often decide whether the
contribution and significance are recognized quickly.

## 5. Compression without loss

A Letter must survive strong compression: distilled, not truncated.
- one sharply defined main claim,
- only the most necessary evidence in the main text,
- aggressive removal of side paths,
- high information density with preserved readability.
A specialized full-length paper merely shortened into Letter form reads dense but
unpersuasive.

## 6. Significance argued, not assumed

State concretely what changed because of the work: resolves a long-standing/widely
recognized issue, changes the interpretation of an important phenomenon, enables a new
class of measurements/calculations/experiments, reveals a qualitatively new regime or
effect, or is likely to influence multiple ongoing directions. "Of broad interest"
alone is weaker than a concrete account of who cares and why.

## 7. Figures and Supplemental

A few key figures carry the central evidence and read on their own. Supplemental
Material is structural, not optional (it backs each compressed claim).

## 8. Length and layout (Letter convention)

- Historically about 3,750 words / ~4 pages. This forces single-focus, compressed
  derivations, selection of only the most essential evidence, and moving secondary
  material to Supplemental.
- PRL does not use `\section`/`\subsection`. Top-level dividers are
  `\paragraph*{Introduction. ---}`-style markers with an em-rule terminator.
  (Mechanical detail in `style.md`.)

---

## Mapping to levels (what PR-review adds when target is PRL)

- **L0**: broad significance, single main claim, "why now / why a broad audience"
  (§1–§3); concreteness of significance (§6).
- **L1**: compression design / side-path removal (§5); structural Supplemental (§7);
  length budget constrains the architecture (§8).
- **L4**: clarity legible outside the specialty (§3).
- **L6**: length limit, `\paragraph*{}` dividers, self-contained figures (§8).
